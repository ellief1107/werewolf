    HOW TO PLAY?

    Read the rule before starting game!

    Setting Up

    In this game, you can create a room by choosing the number of players and the difficulty or join a room in the game lobby. There should be at least six players in a room. There always have 1 Seer, 1 Doctor, and 2 werewolves and the rest of the players should be Villagers. When game starts, each player is aggsined a role by system. Each player should check their card, but must keep it a serect. You can learn more about what each can do here.

    Gameplay

    The game proceeds in alternating night and day rounds. Begin with nighttime.


    The Night

    At night, the moderator (the system) tells all the players, "Close your eyes." Everyone can not send text in the chat board. When the moderator says, "Werewolves, open your eyes and pick someone to kill." The werewolves can discuss at the chat board which is only visible to werewolves.

    When the werewolves have agreed on a victim, and click the button below that victim. If the werewolves choose different victim, the moderator will ask them choose again. Then the moderator says, " Werewolves, close your eyes."

    Now, the moderator awakens the Doctor and says, "Doctor, would you like to heal?" The Doctor can choose to whether heal the victim or not.

    The moderator says "Seer, open your eyes. Seer, pick someone to ask about." The Seer will choose a player. (Again, it is only revisable to Seer.) The moderator will tell him whether the picked one is wolf or not.

    The moderator says, "Everybody open your eyes; it's daytime." And let's the villager know who has been killed. That person is immediately dead and out of the game. They do not reveal their identity.

    The Day


    For the first day, go around and have everyone introduce themselves through the chat board.(Example: Hey, I'm Matt. I'm the baker here in town, and I'm a villager. Daytime is very simple; all the living players gather in the village and decide who to kill. As soon as a majority of players vote for a particular player to kill, the moderator says "Ok, you're dead."

    There are no restrictions on speech. Any living player can say anything they want -- truth, misdirection, nonsense, or a barefaced lie. Dead players may not speak at all. Similarly, as soon as a majority vote indicates that a player has been chosen to be killed, they are dead. If they want to protest their innocence or reveal some information (like the seer's visions), they must do it before the vote goes through.

    Once a player is killed, night falls and the cycle repeats.

    Winning

    The villagers win if they kill both werewolves.

    The werewolves win if they kill both villagers, doctor and seer.
